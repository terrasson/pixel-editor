How to create a pixel art animation in 5 steps

1

Draw your first frame

Start with the base position of your character or object. This is your keyframe — the starting point for the animation.

2

Add a new frame

Click the + button in the frames panel. You can start from scratch or duplicate the current frame to make small changes.

3

Enable onion skinning

Turn on onion skinning to see the previous frame as a faint overlay. This helps you draw the next position consistently.

4

Preview and adjust FPS

Press Play to preview your animation. Adjust the FPS slider to control the speed — try 8 FPS for a retro feel or 24 FPS for smooth motion.

5

Export as GIF

When you're happy with the result, click Export → GIF. The file downloads directly with no watermarks.

Tips for better pixel art animations

Animation tips for pixel artists

  • → Start with a walk cycle — it's the best exercise for learning pixel animation. 4 to 8 frames is enough for a basic loop.
  • → Use 8–12 FPS for a classic retro feel. Modern pixel art games often use 12 FPS for character animations.
  • → Keep your animations short and loopable. A 4-frame idle animation looks great and is easy to make.
  • → Use "squash and stretch" even in pixel art — a character jumping higher on one frame and squashed on landing reads naturally.
  • → Duplicate a frame and make small changes rather than redrawing from scratch. Consistency is the key to smooth motion.
  • → Preview at full speed often. What looks good frame-by-frame can feel wrong at full speed.

Frequently asked questions

How do I create a pixel art animation online?

Open Pixel Editor, draw your first frame, then click the + button to add more frames. Each frame is an independent drawing. Press Play to preview, then export as GIF when done.

What FPS should I use for pixel art animation?

For a classic retro look, 8–12 FPS works well. 24 FPS (the default) produces smoother animations. Slower FPS suits idle loops; faster FPS suits action or effects.

What is onion skinning in pixel art animation?

Onion skinning shows a transparent overlay of the previous frame while you draw the current one. It helps you keep movements consistent and avoid jumpy transitions.

Can I export my pixel art animation as a GIF?

Yes. Pixel Editor exports animated GIFs directly in the browser. The GIF preserves all your frames and timing. No watermarks, completely free.

How many frames can I create?

There's no hard limit on the number of frames. You can create as many as you need for your animation.
